Pact Dice Character Creation Cheat-Sheet/TLDR

Core Draft Mechanic

You will be selecting cards from pre-arranged tables in order to build a character with them.

Some cards have better quality than others, and you will compete with other players to get the best ones.

There are multiple categories, so it's possible for everyone to be the best at different things- or for one person to end up with all the best cards, and everyone else to end up with very weak characters.

The Draft, Step-by-Step

  1. Cards are laid out to form the six tables; each table contains 3 Supremes (12%), 5 Goods (20%), 5 Moderates (20%) 5 Poors (20%), 5 Awfuls (20%), and 2 (8%) Worsts, shuffled into random positions. The middle card of every table is always a Moderate.
  2. All players simultaneously and secretly select a row or column that they want from one table of their choice.
  3. If everyone selected different rows/columns from each table, they each get the cards they picked. If there are cards that multiple players selected, those players Clash.
  4. All cards that got taken are replaced with face-down cards from the "Unknown" deck. The Unknown deck has a distribution of 1 Supreme (5%), 2 Goods (10%), 3 Moderates (15%), 4 Poors (20%), 5 Awfuls (25%), and 5 Worsts (25%). The average quality of an unknown card is 1.8; better than a Worst, but not as good as an Awful.
  5. Repeat steps 2 through 5 until each player has five cards from each table.

Clashes

First, players get a chance to make a deal. In a deal you may propose any possible split of cards (for example P1 taking a supreme card, and P2 taking the remaining four).

Players draw Unknown cards equal to the number they gave to their opponent, so they will always end up taking five no matter what.

Deals may also include promises about what kind of character you'll play, or what relationships they'll have to other characters.

If the players can't or won't agree to a deal, they go to a Dilemma, which resembles the Prisoner's Dilemma, with players having the chance to get white marks (perks and advantages) or black marks (flaws and detrimental traits).
